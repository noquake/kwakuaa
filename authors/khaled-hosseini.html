    Khaled Hosseini

    The Kite Runner (2003)

    'For you, a thousand times over.'

    This one is going to haunt me for a bit. Loyalty is so admirable at times and often equally, if not more so, unbearable. There's purity in how simple it can be. The ability to put someone before oneself, over and over, no matter how many times they forsake themselves will never tear at my heart. But I wouldn't say this book is about loyalty as much as it is about guilt. This, I'd say, is a book about living with guilt, or at least trying to live with it, and seeing what it (redemption or lack of it) does to us.

    I don't know what I was expecting from this when I first picked it up, but it's been recommended by so many people that it's ironic how I started reading it on a whim (thanks Brian) and finished it in days. At its start the story is deceptively easy to follow, its language straightforward and uncomplicated and the characters as rich and deep as they are distinct and lovable. This was the third 525 find, but we'll definitely be securing our own copy.
