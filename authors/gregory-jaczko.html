    Gregory Jaczko

    Confessions of a Rogue Nuclear Regulator (2019)

    This book was definitely a bit out of left field for me. The main reasons I started it were because it was short and seemed interesting, but I kept on going just because of how well-crafted this narrative really was. It was interesting seeing how those in authoritative positions reacted to these events as they unfolded, and it was equally interesting seeing their motivations at play as well. I'm jealous of the students of his class — must be some interesting lectures.
