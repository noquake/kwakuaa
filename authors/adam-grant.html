    Adam Grant

    Give and Take (2013)

    This book moves up the ranking list more so because of impact and writing style. I truly appreciate the prose and language that was used, it felt right on level for a regular high schooler and there were never any moments in the book where I felt lost or confused or even had to reread for any reason. It was super easy to pick up on the go.

    I usually hesitate to read non-fiction just because of the nature of the genre — anything you can't say in an essay is just extra. But with the extra space the writer is truly free to express not only their point, but further counter arguments and everything else surrounding their points, not only making it stronger and more impressive but I felt like I was beginning to create my own opinion instead of simply observing the process on the outside.

    The book made me rethink my giving styles. If I'm being completely honest I feel like I'm very much a matcher — I really hope that I can push myself to be more of a giver, not just because it feels good but because it's honestly more helpful.
