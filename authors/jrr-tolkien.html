    The Hobbit (1937)

    I read this book once before, probably in middle school, and honestly it was pretty great. One thing I liked the most about it was the folk-tale narration of the story. It was whimsical and very much like reading a reading of a story, which is essentially what the book was.

    It's only after reading this book that I can really appreciate how cool this whole world is. It's vast, the existence of magic and mythical beings is treated as fact. The world's history is old and contains details that elude the narrator's omniscient gaze and serves to deepen its flavor. Every creature has its ups and downs. The dwarves are innovative, proud and creative but are weighed down by their value of things and their worship of material value. The elves are fleeting and ephemeral as a people. Men, like in life, seem to be simple and individually underwhelming but together can be formidable. Then the Hobbits are like bigfoot, elusive and barely exist anymore. The point is, there are dimensions to everything and everyone and it was a pleasure to read all about it. Looking forward to LOTR.
