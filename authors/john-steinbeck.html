    John Steinbeck

    Cannery Row (1945)

    A friend of mine recommended this book, and what stood out to me most about her description was how well Steinbeck captured California. In all its heat and haze, the simmering commotion, and the intersection of the different lives in the community.

    The people Steinbeck writes about are written about with such vividness it's impossible not to see them when you look up from the book. I found out later that he based the narrative in a very real area of California — everything from Doc's lab to the junkyard that one weird couple lived in were real, giving substance to an otherwise fictional storyline. I think I'd like to go there someday.
