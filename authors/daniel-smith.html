    Daniel Smith

    Muses, Madmen and Prophets (2007)

    For a nonfiction book that is seemingly focused on the history, study and implications of auditory hallucinations, by chapter 9 this book was on some completely different territory. It feels like the ramblings of some pompous college student during the first draft of their thesis. I can see the language becoming more and more inflated in these later chapters, with pages upon pages of the same content about Socrates which genuinely just worked to piss me off. Did not finish this book and probably never will but I'm glad and relieved that I won't be wasting any more time on it.
