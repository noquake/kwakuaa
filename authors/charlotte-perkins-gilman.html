    Charlotte Perkins Gilman

    The Yellow Wallpaper (1892)

    This was a short story recommended to me by a friend. As someone who doesn't read short stories very often, my expectations were low, but this one didn't disappoint at all. We follow the journal entries of a woman who has just moved into a new house with her family. The only issue is that one room is covered in disgusting, horrid, yellow wallpaper. All the same, our narrator can't help trying to pinpoint what exactly it is about the wallpaper that is disturbing her so much. Slowly she starts falling into a disabling sickness, forcing her to stay inside where she can't escape the very thing that torments her.

    As the pages continue, she becomes more erratic in her descriptions, falling into a sort of madness that can only be described as possession — insane obsession. It's the kind of thing I love seeing in movies and now love to read.
