    Richard Koch

    The 80/20 Principle (1997)

    '20 percent of efforts leading to 80 percent of the outcome' was the calling card of this work, and my first impression was that 1) work 5x as hard or 2) exploit the 20% with the greatest yield and maximize output. I was really glad to realize that Koch focuses on the latter.

    I found this book enjoyable or at least interesting mainly for its attempt to apply this concept to places outside of the workplace or managerial positions. Koch believes that this principle can reform our social lives as well, and lead to a 'time revolution' where one is radicalized against the expected or usual uses of their time — harshly cutting back on waste and revamping how they utilize the same 24 hours a day.

    Personally, I didn't find this advice fully applicable in all parts of my life (I should add that Koch warned of readers having this very thought, so it seems I have some thinking to do). But I found myself engrossed on this novel approach to relationships in the professional and personal space.
