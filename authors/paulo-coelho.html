    Paulo Coelho

    The Alchemist (1988)

    This book was much more like a folktale than I was expecting it to be. It made me think a lot about how I approach the problems in my own life. Things aren't supposed to be easy, especially when it comes to doing the things you want the most in the world.

    If there's one thing I'm glad I picked up from this book, it's the fact that I should listen to my gut. Listen to the heart. I'm still unsure of whether or not we all really have our own 'personal legends' because I still very much subscribe to the whole everything-is-random ideology, but I think nothing can really be accomplished if people give up too easily or if they are too comfortable with letting their dreams be dreams and hesitate to take the steps to realizing them. It was definitely something I needed to read at that time.
