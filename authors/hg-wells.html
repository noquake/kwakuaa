    H.G. Wells

    The Time Machine (1895)

    I still can't believe this was written in 1895. It seriously blows my mind. I can see the DNA of this story in so much of the sci-fi I've grown up with and fallen in love with. What stood out most to me about the way it was written was the emotional aspect of the story. Even in 1895 the line between science and philosophy must have been slightly less blurred than they are now. It was a very unique take on time travel — not sure if I've read or seen any like it.
