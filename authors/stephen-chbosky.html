    Stephen Chbosky

    The Perks of Being a Wallflower (1999)

    This book was genuinely pretty powerful. I remember not only enjoying the different characters presented and how extremely dynamic and grounded they were but always just something a bit off about them. Charlie was definitely a bit of a weird perspective to read from. I found it fun to look at the world and his relationships through his eyes, especially those of his friends and family — it was genuinely interesting to read.

    One thing that has stuck with me from this book was the insanity that was its twist ending. The same aunt that was being referenced the entire time was literally a part of the problem. I ended up reading this book in a day just because of how good it was. Thanks, Beth.
