    Siobhan Adcock

    The Completionist (2018)

    This was by far one of the more recently published books I've read, and it read like it as well. This book was a laundry find so I went in not knowing what to expect apart from the short online description I found. The story was a very pleasant surprise, a new and uniquely modern take on the classic dystopian setting. This time, our modern civilization is crippled not solely because of wars or some other easily recognizable threat but in the infertility of mankind. In this story, one of the most critical plot points is the inception of a child since natural births are so rare in this world. Adcock preserves the modern difficulties of family in a world where they are on the decline. The love of a father, the adoration of a sister, and the loyalty of a brother in a country that is robbed of it all. It's especially interesting considering recent events nowadays.

    Something unique about this one was the mystery. We spend the book trying to understand the disappearance of Gard through the active investigation of the protagonist, but also the rediscovery of the texts and email preceding her disappearance. Both chronological lines end at the climax of the book, making for a uniquely satisfying conclusion. Cool concept, reminds me of Memento.
