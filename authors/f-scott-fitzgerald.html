    F. Scott Fitzgerald

    Tender is the Night (1934)

    This book was work for a bit. I truly wanted to get through it, but I think it was worth it. For some of the same reasons I really enjoyed The Sun Also Rises, Tender is the Night was at times a sad but very real look at what love looked like for the Lost Generation. There is a kind of unclear haze or heat that Fitzgerald brings about when it comes to these seemingly passionate characters that destroy themselves both consciously and unconsciously.

    There's something about self-destruction that I seem to be drawn to as well. I don't find myself necessarily rooting for any characters specifically, but there is still that almost morbid curiosity about finding out if they can find someone just as unbalanced as them to just be okay with, or will they crash and burn, often taking down those closest to them in the process.
