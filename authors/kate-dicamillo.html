    The Tale of Despereaux (2003)

    I adore this book. It reminds me of The Alchemist in its lighthearted nature. I remember it from the time Da used to read to us. I have little to no recollection of him reading it, but it reads so differently now from how I remember. Now reading it a little older, there are some things that are deceptively disturbing like the cauliflower ears or the pitch-black hearts of the rats, but Despereaux's plight to save the princess doesn't seem nearly as central to the story as it once was.

    If anything, my focus was the sub-plot of soup and the significance of the warmth it provides. Every character is just seeking something to hold them over and make them feel safe. Rosauro's love of light, the cook's yearn for soup again, Despereaux's dream of knighthood and adoration of the young Pea. I'm going to hold on to this one for a while. It's sweet.
