    Immanuel Kant

    Prolegomena to Any Future Metaphysics (1783)

    This was the hardest book I've read in a while. As I understand it now, metaphysics is the study of the nature of reality/universe we inhabit. In the pursuit of this science, philosophers create axioms based on what is called 'pure reason'. Pure reason as opposed to regular reasoning is drawn from a special type of observation or knowledge that can be drawn before experiences in the real world. Once you experience and do something the knowledge you get is subjective in a way and I guess not viable for drawing the true laws of metaphysics.

    That's about all I can confidently say I got from the book, though there were parts where I could feel the beginning of an understanding bubbling. I think I'll hold onto it for the next read.
