    P.D. James

    Unnatural Causes (1967)

    This book sits somewhere between despair and conceited depravity, all wrapped in some cozy atmosphere by James's prose and careful dialogue. I burned through this story in about a week and I've realized that not all books need to carry some weighted message or somehow change the course of my life. This one felt like finishing one film during a movie marathon. Though distinct in its plot and feeling, once over it all seemed somewhat underwhelming — like a minor episode in a long-running series.

    First from the stack of books from 525 which I will be working on this summer. I think I've become a minor fan of P.D. James. I could find myself reading a couple more of these Dalgliesh stories. They remind me of what I think things may have been like before everything was stream-able from anywhere. I would recommend this to any fan of crime and reasonably eccentric characters. Fun, in all its grim, grey glory.
