    Agatha Christie

    And Then There Were None (1939)

    There's no reason why this book should have taken me so long to finish but I was truly just procrastinating so I feel kinda bad. I really enjoyed this book though, it was an interesting read. There was a good amount of tension throughout the book however, I truly enjoyed the character interactions and the interesting twists and turns and abrupt deaths — fun to see. I really thought I'd be more able to follow the plot and try to find the murderer when in reality each of the ten people on the island died and I had no idea how it happened.

    My main reason for reading this book was because it was the 'start' of its genre, it created this trope of people dying one by one for no apparent reason so it's always important to visit the original material. One thing that'll stick with me is how abrupt the deaths are.
