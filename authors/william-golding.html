    William Golding

    Lord of the Flies (1954)

    I found this book to be enjoyable although somewhat boring at times. I think this book had some important themes and morals being taught. It really helped me understand why children are such scumbags and to a certain extent I do believe that they were doomed from the start, honestly just because of the mix of kids that they were forced to work with. Jack was such a fucking shithead and I was really hoping he would die at some point.

    The powers at play in this book — the littluns vs the biguns, hunters and non-hunters — it's all barbarism and the group mentality just stems from a bit of undeserved pride and catches fire, to which kids are especially vulnerable. If anything the littluns just acted as catalysts for that group mentality reaction, quick to rally and just as quick to disband.
