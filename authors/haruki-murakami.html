    Haruki Murakami

    Norwegian Wood (1987)

    There is something so simple about this book that it feels as if it could only be read one way. It's so clear in fact that all of its disturbing elements are even more so disturbing. I felt, while reading it, that there were some aspects I could almost directly relate to — romance, loneliness, academic pressures — but now I'm starting to think that this book was far beyond me in more than a few ways.

    I'm fortunate enough to never have experienced a close death yet, but the other aspects of mental illness and the sexual parts as well as certain relationships genuinely weirded me out. Regardless, it was easily one of the best written books I've read recently, and I'd love to have a reread to delve into it deeper sometime soon.
