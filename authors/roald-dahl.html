    Roald Dahl

    The Landlady (1959)

    From the mind that created Charlie and the Chocolate Factory, we now read a short story that can only be described as deeply disturbing or at least unsettling. A young man's attention is drawn to the charming owner of an inn, who, I would add, is clearly the devil. The story recounts the young man peeling back the layers of the inn, the woman, and realizing that things are not as they seem. An enjoyable read — will look into more horror soon, maybe it's time for a King novel.
