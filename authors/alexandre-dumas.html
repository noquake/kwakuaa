    Alexandre Dumas

    The Black Tulip (1850)

    It was a really pleasant surprise to find out that Dumas was black. Outside of that, this was a really nice little romantic story. I do feel a bit bad that I read it in English instead of French, but this way I can get the other language later. I remember the endearing romantic struggle between our two lead roles — an admirable duo with an almost whimsical air stemming from tragic, even horrific roots. Would give this to anyone looking for a good story before bed who is a fan of long, French sentences.
