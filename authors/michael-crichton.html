    Michael Crichton

    Sphere (1987)

    The story centers around 53-year-old psychologist Norman Johnson. Years ago he was called in by the ministry of defense to create the U.L.F. contact team, a hand-picked group of individuals perfectly capable of collaborating under extreme stress in contacting unknown life forms. They are transported 1,000 feet below sea level in the Pacific where they investigate a mysterious vessel, half a mile long and hollow.

    This book has been a longtime favorite of mine ever since grandma found it outside her church, and I'm glad I've been able to keep it in such good condition. This book is one that really spurred me on to continue reading throughout my life and I plan to reread it again in the future. Every aspect of this book is amazing to me — having been written in the 80s where a lot of entertainment tropes were just starting, especially from an author like Crichton who originated so many of them.
