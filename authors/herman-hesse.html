    Hermann Hesse

    Demian (1919)

    This book was an experience. Soon after reading it, I remember calling it a 'dark epic' or some sort of twisted coming-of-age or rise to power story. We follow the tale of a young man finding the place of religion in the world as he gradually comes to understand that people are far more complex than any right/wrong description can describe. All this change is sparked by the boy-genius named Demian. As he grows older and learns more about this organization/cult/religion that is uniting minds like theirs, the less of myself I saw in the protagonist. There was a lot more to go into, but I'll save that for the reread and paper.
