    Harlan Ellison

    I Have No Mouth, and I Must Scream (1967)

    This novel was a serious trip. I was surprised at the level of detail painted in the few pages of the narrative, also at the sheer pain that was expressed. It genuinely had some of the darkest aspects of sci-fi I could imagine — virtual reality, time manipulation, and some inherent ethical dilemmas. There was a lot going on. I think I need to get into the horror side, because if there are movies for it, there are definitely more books.
