    Brian W. Aldiss

    Cryptozoic! (1967)

    This is the first book from Aldiss I've ever read. I liked the range of characters but the key of this book is in its interpretation of the nature of the universe. Naisola recommended it to me and I can see why. Imagine that time as we perceive it is inherently backwards. Instead of moving 'forward' as we call it, we'd be moving 'backward'. Being born from 'death' and finding our final end in the wombs of our mothers. While it took a while for me to wrap my head around it, there are more resolutions for this way of thinking than I had initially let on. The key for most problems is to understand the fact that the 'over mind' which is the higher level of thinking that skews our vision of reality, making us falsely believe that we as observers of reality are not active participants in its actions. Understanding that the under mind, the primitive most natural part of us is the true human nature trying to reconcile with what we already understand to be the nature of things. We would trade the hope of things to happen with the certainty of what we know will occur. We start old and near death and progressively degenerate becoming younger and stronger before becoming one with other human beings. Aldiss used some strong imagery to explain these interactions nearing the ending of the book and I can say that those were among my favorite parts to read. My only gripe was the ending I don't know why he chose to have such a lackluster finish. While I didn't expect to really see the rise of the real humanity I can't believe he reduced the book to being the ramblings of a lunatic..?
