    Frank Herbert

    Children of Dune (1976)

    This book was great but got grating near the end. I found Alia's insanity driven by her 'abominafication' kind of insane, though I was a big fan of the tearing apart of the family. I thought it was really cool how big of a role Stilgar played — despite being a family of 'gods' and beings with otherworldly powers, everyone needs a friend and it was cool to see, even if he was kind of forced at every turn. Will be taking a break from the series for a while.

    Dune Messiah (1969)

    It took me a while to read this book, and once I did I feel like I sped through it without truly enjoying it. This was the last book to contain Paul and after he left I found it difficult to adjust to the series. In this book we see Paul fully realized as the emperor and dealing with the new issues he has as everything he said would pass has happened, so now what? It was a cool read, and seeing Alia grow up was interesting — as always there was amazing imagery and writing but I honestly finished feeling underwhelmed.

    Dune (1965)

    This level of complexity, depth and maturity in a book can only be referred to as a masterpiece. This book was insane from start to end. Not only was the setting nothing like I've ever seen before — and this isn't just talking about the planet Arrakis but the general time period and the stature of society — but the characters that called it home, the organizations like the Bene Gesserit, the relationships between the houses and then the empire itself. The span of time the events of this first book covers and the amount of worldbuilding it does to justify them are interesting and impressive for one person to have thought of.
