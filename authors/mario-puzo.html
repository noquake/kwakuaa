    Mario Puzo

    The Godfather (1969)

    A gripping book, honestly. A level of immersion that kept me completely focused in the middle of parks and cities during Spring break. It is surprising how much of an air Puzo creates with what he chooses to describe and how he does it. We are placed in a society where respect and cunning play such a high-stakes game that aspects a reader would regularly glance over need to be inspected and reinspected lest they lead to the death of a major character. In some ways, the book arguably creates more of an atmosphere of tension and pressure than the movie itself. I look forward to eating through The Sicilian and other books from Puzo in the future.
